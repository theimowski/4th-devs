Jeremy Howard (born November 13, 1973) is a prominent Australian data scientist, entrepreneur, and educator. He is best known as the co-founder of fast.ai and for his significant contributions to making deep learning more accessible to a global audience.

Early Life and Education

Birth: Born in London, England; moved to Melbourne, Australia, in 1976.
Education: Attended Melbourne Grammar and studied Philosophy at the University of Melbourne.
Early Interests: Contributed to major open-source projects including the Perl programming language (chair of the Perl6-data working group), Cyrus IMAP server, and Postfix SMTP server.

Key Contributions

Technical Innovations

ULMFiT ( Universal Language Model Fine-tuning ): Developed this algorithm which pioneered transfer learning and fine-tuning in Natural Language Processing (NLP). This work is credited with helping pave the way for modern language models like GPT.

fastai Library: Created a layered API for deep learning that simplifies the implementation of complex neural networks.

Education and Advocacy

Democratizing AI: Through fast.ai, he has trained thousands of practitioners, moving AI from the realm of academic specialists to general software engineers.

Academic Roles: Honorary Professor at the University of Queensland and former Distinguished Research Scientist at the University of San Francisco.

Public Health: During the COVID-19 pandemic, he was a leading global advocate for public masking, co-authoring an influential evidence review published in PNAS.
