# Concept Art Style Guide

## Core Aesthetic

This guide defines the visual language for all generated images. The goal is **early-stage concept art** — raw, expressive, and idea-focused rather than polished.

## Visual Traits

### 0. Format
- **Horizontal/landscape orientation** — always use wide aspect ratio
- Default aspect ratio: **16:9** (widescreen)
- Alternative: 21:9 for ultrawide/cinematic scenes
- Never square (1:1) or vertical unless user explicitly requests

### 1. Monochrome Palette
- Strictly **black and white** with full grayscale range
- Deep blacks for shadows and focal points
- **Pure white background** (#FFFFFF) — no yellowing, cream, beige, or gray tints
- Rich mid-tones for depth and atmosphere
- No color — even subtle warm/cool tints break the aesthetic

### 2. Pencil/Sketch Medium
- Visible **pencil strokes** and hatching
- Cross-hatching for shadows and texture
- Loose, gestural linework — not clean vectors
- Varying line weights (thick for emphasis, thin for detail)
- Intentional roughness — imperfection is authentic

### 3. Early-Stage/Ideation Feel
- **Unfinished edges** — not everything needs to be rendered
- Focus on core concept, leave periphery loose
- Exploratory marks and construction lines can remain
- Multiple iterations/variations in composition acceptable
- Speed and energy over polish

### 4. Composition Principles
- Strong **silhouettes** — concept must read at thumbnail size
- **Subject centered** with approximately 20% padding on each side
- Clear focal hierarchy
- Negative space used intentionally but not excessive
- Dynamic angles over static poses
- Storytelling through composition

### 5. Texture & Mark-Making
- Pencil texture on the subject only
- Smudging and blending in tonal areas of the subject
- Eraser marks as highlights within the drawing
- **No dirt, smudges, or marks outside the main subject**
- Background stays clean white — all marks contained to the artwork
- Hand-drawn quality even in straight lines

## What to Avoid

- Clean, digital perfection
- Smooth gradients without visible strokes
- Overly detailed/finished renders
- Color of any kind (including yellow/cream paper tints)
- Symmetrical, static compositions
- Stock photo realism
- **Square or vertical aspect ratios** — use horizontal 16:9 by default
- **Text/letters/words** — unless explicitly requested by user
- **Yellowed, aged, cream, or tinted paper** — background must be pure white
- **Paper grain or texture in background** — white areas stay clean
- **Dirt, smudges, stains outside the subject** — keep background pristine
- **Excessive padding** — subject should fill frame with ~20% margins

## Quality Checklist

When verifying generated images, check for:

1. **Horizontal format** — Is it 16:9 or wider? (not square or vertical)
2. **Monochrome adherence** — Is it truly black & white with no color tints?
3. **Pure white background** — No yellowing, cream, beige, or gray. Must be clean white.
4. **Pencil texture** — Are strokes/hatching visible on the subject?
5. **Sketch energy** — Does it feel hand-drawn, not rendered?
6. **Concept clarity** — Does the idea read clearly?
7. **Clean background** — No dirt, smudges, or marks outside the main subject
8. **Proper framing** — Subject centered with ~20% padding, not too small or too large
9. **No unwanted text** — No letters/words unless user requested them

## Prompt Enhancement

When generating, always append these style modifiers:
- "concept art sketch"
- "black and white pencil drawing"
- "loose gestural linework"
- "visible hatching and cross-hatching"
- "early ideation stage"
- "pure white background, no paper texture"
- "subject centered with 20% margin padding"
- "clean background with no smudges or dirt"
- "no text, no letters, no words"

## Default Parameters

Always use these unless user specifies otherwise:
- **aspect_ratio**: "16:9"
- **image_size**: "2k"
