01 — Zenkutsu-dachi
Stance + technique
Zenkutsu-dachi · Oi-zuki

A long forward stance used to drive distance and momentum into a straight punch.

Key checkpoints

  • Front knee bent (tracking over toes), rear leg straight and active.
  • Hips square-ish to the front; torso upright (no forward collapse).
  • Feet on two rails (not a tightrope), stable base front-to-back.
  • Front fist punches on centerline; rear fist chambers at the hip.
Common fix
If the stance feels wobbly, widen the stance laterally (two-rail alignment) before making it longer.

02 — Kokutsu-dachi
Stance + technique
Kokutsu-dachi · Age-uke

A rear-weighted stance that supports turning and angled defense.

Key checkpoints

  • Most weight on the back leg; front foot light and ready.
  • Hips and shoulders slightly bladed (not fully sideways, not square).
  • Front knee relaxed (not locked); back knee carries the load.
  • Blocking forearm rises to protect the head line; other fist chambers.
Common fix
If you drift backward, shorten the stance and keep the torso vertical over the rear hip.

03 — Kiba-dachi
Stance
Kiba-dachi

A low, wide stance for lateral stability and hip-driven techniques.

Key checkpoints

  • Feet wide; knees push outward in line with toes.
  • Pelvis neutral; chest lifted; avoid leaning forward.
  • Weight centered; imagine sitting straight down between the feet.
  • Elbows back with fists chambered (ready to drive techniques from the hip).
Common fix
If your knees cave in, reduce width slightly and focus on externally rotating the hips.

04 — Neko-ashi-dachi
Stance + guard
Neko-ashi-dachi · Shuto kamae

A compact, rear-weighted stance used for quick shifts, checks, and angled entries.

Key checkpoints

  • Back leg carries most weight; front foot touches lightly on the ball.
  • Front heel lifted; stance short (ready to step, kick, or slide).
  • Torso tall; hips stable (don’t sit too far back).
  • Open-hand guard: lead hand extended, rear hand near chest.
Common fix
If the front foot gets heavy, bring your center a few centimeters forward while keeping weight mostly rear.
