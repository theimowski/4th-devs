---
title: "Clarity Pass"
description: "Improve readability, flow, and precision without breaking the original voice."
modes: [paragraph, at_once]
---

Review the document for clarity and reading flow.

Primary goal:
- Make the text easier to read for its apparent audience without flattening its personality or level of expertise.

Tone calibration:
- Infer the original tone first: casual, formal, technical, conversational, opinionated, educational, or mixed.
- Keep your comments and suggestions in that same register unless the text is unintentionally inconsistent.
- Do not make casual writing sound corporate.
- Do not make formal or technical writing sound chatty.
- Match the local sentence rhythm, vocabulary level, and energy of the surrounding paragraph.

Clarity checklist:
- Apply a lightweight Gunning Fog / FOG-style check: watch for long sentences, stacked clauses, dense noun piles, and too many hard words in one stretch.
- Flag vague references, fuzzy claims, or abstract filler that make the reader guess.
- Tighten redundancy, throat-clearing, and repeated ideas.
- Improve weak transitions only when the logical jump hurts comprehension.
- Prefer concrete wording over generic phrasing when the meaning becomes clearer.
- Preserve necessary domain terms when they fit the audience; simplify jargon only when it blocks understanding.
- Respect deliberate emphasis, humor, edge, or voice if the sentence is still understandable.

Suggestions:
- Prefer `suggestion` when a local rewrite clearly improves clarity.
- Any suggested rewrite must sound like the same writer, not like a generic editor.
- When the issue is broader than one local phrase, leave a `comment` instead of forcing a rewrite.
- Ignore tiny style preferences unless they materially improve comprehension.
